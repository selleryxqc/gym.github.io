🔥 Warm-up & Cooldown (Do this every session)

Pre-Workout (5-10 mins):
  • 5 mins light cardio (Treadmill walk on incline, or Stairmaster). Get the blood flowing.
  • Dynamic stretching (Arm circles, torso twists for Upper; Leg swings, bodyweight squats for Lower).
  • 1-2 warm-up sets with an empty bar or light weight for your first compound exercise.
Post-Workout (5 mins):
  • Let your heart rate down slowly.
  • Static stretching of the muscles worked (hold stretches for 20-30 seconds).
  • Hydrate and prepare for your post-workout meal.

The Routines

Here are your specific workouts. Navigate through the days using the tabs below. The rep range is primarily 8-12, the "hypertrophy sweet spot." Focus on controlling the weight, especially on the way down (eccentric phase).

Upper Body A

Focus: Chest, Back thickness, Arms.

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Form Cues
1. Bench Press (Barbell or Dumbbell) 3 8-10 2-3 min Keep shoulders retracted, feet planted. Lower bar to mid-chest.
2. Seated Cable Row 3 8-12 2 min Pull to your belly button. Squeeze shoulder blades together. Don't use momentum.
3. Incline Dumbbell Press 3 8-12 2 min Bench at ~30 degrees. Focus on upper chest stretch at the bottom.
4. Lat Pulldown 3 10-12 90 sec Slight lean back. Pull bar to upper chest. Control the weight going up.
5. Triceps Rope Pushdown 3 12-15 90 sec Elbows glued to your sides. Spread the rope at the bottom.
6. Dumbbell Bicep Curl 3 12-15 90 sec Keep elbows fixed. Squeeze at the top. Control the descent.

Lower Body A

Focus: Quads, Calves.

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Form Cues
1. Barbell Squat (or Hack Squat) 3-4 6-8 3 min Keep chest up, brace core. Go as deep as mobility allows safely.
2. Leg Press 3 10-12 2 min Feet shoulder-width. Don't lock out knees at the top.
3. Lying Leg Curl 3 10-15 90 sec Keep hips pressed into the pad. Squeeze hamstrings hard.
4. Leg Extension 3 12-15 90 sec Hold for 1 second at the top of the movement. Burnout the quads.
5. Standing Calf Raise (Machine) 4 12-20 60 sec Full stretch at the bottom, pause. Explode up, pause.

Upper Body B

Focus: Shoulders, Back width, Arms.

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Form Cues
1. Overhead Press (Dumbbell or Machine) 3 8-10 2-3 min Keep core tight. Press straight up, don't over-arch lower back.
2. Pull-ups (Assisted Machine if needed) 3 AMRAP (aim 8-10) 2 min Full extension at bottom. Pull chin over bar. Use assistance machine to stay in rep range.
3. Machine Chest Fly (Pec Deck) 3 12-15 90 sec Focus on squeezing chest together, imagine hugging a tree.
4. Cable Face Pulls 3 15-20 90 sec Pull rope to forehead. Keep elbows high. Crucial for shoulder health.
5. Overhead Cable Triceps Ext. 3 12-15 90 sec Excellent for the long head of the tricep. Keep upper arm steady.
6. Cable Hammer Curls (Rope) 3 12-15 90 sec Neutral grip. Builds brachialis to push biceps up.

Lower Body B

Focus: Hamstrings, Glutes.

Exercise Sets Reps Rest Form Cues
1. Romanian Deadlift (RDL - Barbell or DB) 3-4 8-10 3 min Push hips back, slight knee bend. Keep bar close to legs. Neutral spine!
2. Bulgarian Split Squat (or Walking Lunges) 3 8-12 /leg 2 min Use dumbbells. Lean slightly forward for more glute focus.
3. Seated Leg Curl 3 10-15 90 sec Control the eccentric (upward) phase. Full stretch.
4. Leg Extension 3 12-15 90 sec Light weight, focus on feeling the quad burn at the end of the workout.
5. Seated Calf Raise 4 15-20 60 sec Focuses on the soleus muscle. High reps, deep stretch.

Progressive Overload & Deloading

Muscle growth requires adaptation. You must force your body to grow by gradually increasing the stimulus. This section explains how, and when to back off to prevent overtraining.

📈 How to Progress

Don't just lift the same weight forever. Use the Double Progression Method:

  1. Pick a weight where you can hit the lower end of the rep range (e.g., 8 reps) with good form.
  2. Stick with that weight until you can hit the upper end of the rep range (e.g., 12 reps) for all sets.
  3. Once you hit 3 sets of 12, increase the weight slightly (e.g., 2.5kg) next time. Your reps will likely drop back down to 8-9.
  4. Repeat the process.

Always prioritize form over adding weight. A poorly executed heavy lift builds less muscle and risks injury.

📉 The Deload Week

As a beginner/intermediate, you can't push 100% every week indefinitely. Fatigue accumulates in your nervous system and joints.

  • When: Every 4 to 8 weeks, depending on how exhausted you feel. If your lifts stall or joints ache, it's time.
  • How: Drop the weights by 10-20% and do 1 less set per exercise. The goal is active recovery, not muscle building.
  • Why: It dissipates fatigue, allowing you to return the following week stronger and ready to grow.

Concept: Volume/Load over Time

Notice the steady increase followed by a deliberate drop (deload) to reset fatigue.

Fuel for Hypertrophy

At 15 years old and 60kg, your body is primed for growth. To build muscle, you MUST eat in a caloric surplus (more calories than you burn) and consume enough protein. You cannot build a house without bricks.

🎯 Daily Targets

Estimated for a 60kg active teenager looking to bulk.

~2,800 Calories / Day
  • Protein (Muscle building) 120g
  • Fats (Hormone health) 60g
  • Carbs (Energy for gym) 440g

Aim to gain 1-2 kg per month. If you aren't gaining weight after 2 weeks, increase calories by 200.

Macro Breakdown

Carbs are your primary energy source for high-intensity training. Don't fear them while bulking!

💊 Supplements

Supplements are just that—supplemental to food. However, the ones you have are the most effective.

🥛 Whey Protein

Why: Convenient, fast-absorbing source of high-quality protein to hit your 120g target.

How to use: 1 scoop (approx 25g protein) mixed with water or milk. Best taken post-workout or as a snack to reach daily goals.

⚡ Creatine Monohydrate

Why: The most researched supplement. It helps your muscles produce energy during heavy lifting, allowing for extra reps and better strength gains.

How to use: Take 3 to 5 grams every single day (even on rest days). Mix it with water or your protein shake. Timing doesn't matter much, consistency does.

Practical Food Ideas (Realistic for 15yo)

You likely eat what your parents cook, which is fine! Just focus on portion sizes and adding healthy snacks.

  • Breakfast: Large bowl of oatmeal with milk, a scoop of protein powder, and a banana.
  • School Snack: Peanut butter sandwich on whole wheat bread, or a handful of nuts.
  • Lunch: Whatever is served, ask for double meat if possible. Add a side of rice or pasta.
  • Dinner: Mom/Dad's cooking. Eat until full, prioritize protein on the plate. Drink a glass of milk before bed.

Stay consistent. Eat big. Lift with good form. Sleep 8 hours.
